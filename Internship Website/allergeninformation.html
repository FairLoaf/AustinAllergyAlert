Allergen Descriptions

Below you will find specific information about the most common allergens in Austin. If you would like to request information on another allergen, please email us using the information in the Contact page.


Mold


Source: Grows from tiny spores in areas with a lot of moisture.

Cedar


Source: Pollen released in the air by Cedar trees.

Oak


Source: Pollen released in the air by Oak trees.

Ash


Source: Pollen released in the air by Ash trees.

Grass


Source: Comes from taller grasses which grow small flowers on top, may help to keep grasses cut short. Specifically, made by Bermuda Grass and Johnson Grass.

Mulberry


Source: Pollen released in the air by Mulberry trees.

Pecan


Source: Pollen released in the air by Pecan trees and is spread quickly from wind.

Elm


Source: Pollen released in the air by the fruits and flowers that are released from the Elm tree.
